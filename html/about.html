What is Jack ?

Jack is a sophisticated web browser extension designed to enhance your online security and convenience.

With Jack, you can easily create and manage temporary email addresses, protecting your primary inbox from spam and unwanted messages.

In addition, Jack offers a robust local password management service. This lightweight tool ensures that all your passwords are securely stored on your device, providing quick and safe access to your accounts without relying on external servers.

User guide

Here is a quick user guide to enhance your experience with Jack!

Temorary emails addresses

Jack allows you to easily and quickly create disposable email addresses. These email addresses can only be used to receive emails. The received emails are stored for up to one week before being deleted; however, the email address remains active until you delete it.

The page for managing your temporary email addresses is the default page displayed by the extension after you log in. To access it, simply click on the extension icon in your browser

Create a new address

To create a new address, simply click on the + button at the top right, then enter the desired name for your address (do not include the domain name, it is generated automatically), then click Generate. You can also leave the name field blank to generate a random name.

Access your mailbox

After generating an email address, click on its name to access the inbox. You will be able to see all the emails you have received in the last 7 days.

Quick use

When you want to use your temporary email address on the web, you can quickly use it by right-clicking on the desired field and selecting Jack then Use temporary email. This will automatically fill the field with a temporary email address.

Password manager

Jack offers an intuitive and lightweight password manager. You can access it by clicking on at the top right. The password manager opens only as a browser tab and not in the extension popup.

Security

Your passwords and all associated information are stored encrypted using the AES-128 algorithm. The encryption key is not stored permanently but is generated from your master password. Therefore, the security of your passwords depends on the strength of your master password.

Your information is all stored locally on your computer, and Jack does not communicate with any external servers.

Save a password

To save a new password, click on the + button at the top right of the password manager page. Then fill in the desired fields. The Title and Password fields are mandatory.

The URL field is also useful if you want to automatically fill in your login credentials on the website associated with this URL.

You can generate a random password using password generators, which will be presented in the next section. Select the generator you want to use and click on to generate a random password.

Password generators

Password generators are tools that allow you to generate a random password based on several predefined criteria. Generators can be created, modified, and deleted from the application's settings page, which will be presented in the next section.

To configure a new password generator, go to the extension settings by clicking on at the top right of the page, then navigate to the Password generators section and click on the + button to create a generator.
Then configure the generator by filling in the fields:

  • Name : Your generator's name.
  • Password length : The number of characters in the password.
  • Excluded characters : A list of characters that are excluded for password generation.
  • Allowed characters : The types of characters used for password generation.

Then click on Save.

You can also delete a generator by clicking on and edit it by clicking on its name.

There is a default generator that cannot be deleted; however, it can be modified.

Quick use

You can quickly use your login credentials on a website by right-clicking on a form field, then clicking on Jack and selecting Use saved credentials.
Jack will search your saved passwords for the one that best matches the URL you are on and will automatically fill in the fields.

Settings

The settings page is accessible by clicking on .

Change the master password

You can change your master password, which allows access to the application, by clicking on Change Master Password. Be sure to use a secure password! A strength indicator will show you how long it might take for your password to be discovered by brute force.

Change the session validity time

You can change the validity period of your session, which corresponds to the duration after which you will be logged out of the application if no activity is detected from you on the browser.

Data Import/Export

You can export all the data stored on your Jack Extension using the Export Local Data feature. This will generate a file, with the name you have chosen, containing all the data stored locally by Jack. This data will be protected by your password.

You can import data to any instance of Jack by selecting the file generated by the exporter and clicking on .
You will be asked for the file password to decrypt the data.
Your master password will be replaced by the one stored in the imported file's data. You can keep your current password by checking Keep.

Reset Jack

You can completely reset the extension to default settings. All your data will be deleted and unrecoverable.

Frequenlty Asked Questions

Here you will find a list of frequently asked questions, to which we will try to provide the most accurate answers possible.

Is Jack really secure for my passwords ?

Jack stores all data locally. Since nothing goes online, many threats are neutralized. To protect your data locally, Jack encrypts your data with the AES-128 algorithm, which is known for its security. The encryption key is not kept in memory but is generated on-the-fly during your login. Your master password is hashed using the SHA-512 algorithm with salt, which is considered secure.
Jack takes every measure to keep your data as secure as possible.

Can I send emails with Jack ?

No, you can only receive emails with this extension.

Can I use Jack without main password ?

No, this extension is not designed to be used without a password. However, there are no constraints on the strength and length of the password, so you can use a simple password if you wish, although it is not recommended.
